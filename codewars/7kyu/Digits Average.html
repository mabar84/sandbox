Given an integer, take the (mean) average of each pair of consecutive digits. Repeat this process until you have a single integer, then return that integer. e.g. Note: if the average of two digits is not an integer, round the result up (e.g. the average of 8 and 9 will be 9) Examples digitsAverage(246) ==> 4 original: 2 4 6 \ / \ / 1st iter: 3 5 \ / 2nd iter: 4 digitsAverage(89) ==> 9 original: 8 9 \ / 1st iter: 9 p.s. for a bigger challenge, check out the one line version of this kata by myjinxin2015!
